## Overview

[FILL IN] Cycads are an ancient group of seed plants (gymnosperms) in the division Cycadophyta. They are the sole surviving members of a lineage that dates back at least 280 million years to the Permian period, making them among the oldest seed plants on Earth. Sometimes called "living fossils", cycads dominated many Mesozoic landscapes during the age of the dinosaurs.

Today, approximately **350 species** across **10 genera** and **3 families** survive, mostly in tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. All cycads are dioecious (separate male and female plants) and characteristically bear stout trunks crowned with pinnate (or, uniquely in *Bowenia*, bipinnate) fronds and produce large cones. They are among the **most threatened plant groups in the world**, with the majority of species listed as Threatened on the IUCN Red List.

## Key Characteristics

- **Ancient lineage:** fossil record from the Permian (~280 Ma)
- **Dioecious:** separate male and female plants
- **Cones (strobili):** reproduction via cones, not flowers
- **Coralloid roots:** specialised roots hosting nitrogen-fixing cyanobacteria (*Nostoc* spp.)
- **Cycasin toxicity:** all parts of cycads contain cycasin and other neurotoxins
- **Slow growth:** extremely long-lived; some individuals estimated over 1,000 years old

## Distribution

[FILL IN] Cycads occur naturally in a broad belt around the tropics and subtropics, with centres of diversity in:

- **The Americas** — *Zamia*, *Ceratozamia*, *Dioon*, *Microcycas*
- **Africa** — *Encephalartos*, *Stangeria*
- **Asia and the Pacific** — *Cycas*
- **Australia** — *Macrozamia*, *Lepidozamia*, *Bowenia*

---

## Conservation

[FILL IN] Cycads are considered the world's most threatened plant group. Key threats include:

- Habitat destruction and land conversion
- Illegal collection for horticultural trade
- Traditional medicine harvesting
- Slow reproduction rates

All cycad species are listed on [CITES](https://cites.org) Appendix I or II, restricting international trade.
